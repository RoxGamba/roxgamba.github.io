On the Easter Monday, I did something much better than celebrating pasquetta with my friends (sorry guys, next time for sure): I gave a seminar at the [University of Virginia](https://www.phys.virginia.edu/) about horizon fluxes on non-circularized orbits. For my 25 readers[^1], slides are publicly available [here](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1AumHTE1T3s6uLH6IKZxXiPiuy8xjUNwqNAdJXNd9IYg/edit?usp=sharing), while the related papers are available on arXiv [here](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.15322) and [here](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28982)


[Indranil](https://fulbrightscholars.org/olf/indranil-chakraborty) was an awesome host, and Charlottesville rather charming. I also got to meet with [Kent](https://kentyagi27.wixsite.com/website), [Adam](https://inspirehep.net/authors/1040952?ui-citation-summary=true&ui-exclude-self-citations=true), [David](https://dnichols1.github.io/) and the broader gravity reserch group.


All in all, a great visit and I hope to be back soon. To quote a friend of mine, "Virginia is for science lovers"! 


[^1]: 25 is a gross overestimation. If you don't get the reference, it probably means you have not had the pleasure (?) of reading ``I promessi sposi'' in high school... And honestly, good for you.
